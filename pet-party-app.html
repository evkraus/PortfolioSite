The goal of this project was to demonstrate my knowledge and skills in UI/UX, to create a user-centered high fidelity prototype of a “useless” app. Although called useless, this app would have a target audience, and be generally useless to the masses. This was done using various research methods and then creation within Figma. The app is a clicker game called Pet Party. Clicker-style, meaning the player interacts with the game by clicking repeatedly to advance.

I started the research process by sketching three different app concepts on paper and then narrowing them down based off pros and cons. I identified a potential problem the app could address, as well as possible solutions to that problem. Using these ideas, I conducted a survey among 10 students, which helped refine the app’s purpose and allowed me to create a user persona. Through the survey, I learned that people find interacting with animals/pets and using simple colors to be relaxing, which significantly influenced my design choices later on.

Once I got to the wireframing/design phase I initially created paper wireframes and had a student test it as if it was an actual app to air out any design or functionality problems. I then did wireframing in Figma focusing on layout and look of major elements, such as placement, size and look of them. After receiving feedback from multiple students, it was brought to my attention that some elements coukd be too small for some people to see. I then went back and revised.

For the high-fidelity pototyping portion I went into Figma and gathered all the resources such as images and colours. I began by designing each screen referring constantly to the medium fidelity mockups. I then moved onto the interactivity, this step was hardest as it was my first project with Figma. To combat this i left additional time for this step as well as additional tests among classmates, to then revise multiple times.
